Why School Doesn’t Teach You How to Get Rich

1. School Prepares You for a Job, Not Wealth

Schools were created to train people to follow rules and work in offices or factories. That’s why you learn to sit still, be on time, and do as you're told. But to get rich, you need to take risks, be creative, and think differently—things school rarely teaches.

2. They Don’t Teach Money Skills

Most schools don’t show you how to handle money, invest, or build assets. They may teach how to get a job and earn, but not how to grow money or become financially free.

3. Teachers Don’t Know Either

Many teachers aren’t trained in building wealth either. They follow the same system—study hard, get a job. So they can’t teach what they don’t know.

4. Talking About Money Feels Wrong

In many schools, talking about getting rich seems greedy or wrong. So they focus on safe careers and avoid the topic of wealth completely.

5. They Teach You to Work for Money, Not Make Money Work for You

Schools teach you to work hard to earn money. But rich people do more—they make money work for them through investing and passive income. School doesn’t show that path.

What Can You Do?

If you want to learn how to get rich, you have to do it yourself:

  • Read books like Rich Dad Poor Dad
  • Watch videos about investing and business
  • Learn from successful people
  • Start saving, investing, and building skills

School may not teach you how to get rich—but you can still learn it. Start now, and take control of your future.
